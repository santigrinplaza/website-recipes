Ñoquis Recipe

Description

Ñoquis are a beloved dish that brings comfort with every bite. Originating from Italy, this Latin American version is made primarily from mashed potatoes mixed with flour, egg, and a bit of salt to create a soft dough. The dough is then shaped into small logs, cut into bite-sized pieces, and sometimes pressed with a fork to form ridges. These ridges help sauces adhere to the ñoquis, making each bite full of flavor. The gentle, pillowy texture of the gnocchi melts in your mouth, offering a warm, satisfying experience.

Once cooked in boiling water, the ñoquis float to the surface, signaling that they're ready to be served. They are often sautéed in butter for a golden finish and garnished with fresh herbs such as basil or sage for added flavor. Though the dish can be paired with a variety of sauces, it’s traditionally enjoyed with a simple butter sauce, allowing the ñoquis to shine as the main star. Perfect for family meals or special occasions, ñoquis offer a delightful and versatile way to enjoy the simple yet rich taste of potatoes.

Ingridients

  • 2 lbs (900g) potatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (for serving)
  • Fresh basil or sage leaves (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. Boil the potatoes: Place the peeled and chopped potatoes in a large pot of water and cook until soft (about 15-20 minutes). Drain the potatoes and mash them until smooth, allowing them to cool slightly.
  2. Make the dough: On a clean surface, combine the mashed potatoes, flour, egg, salt, and optional nutmeg or Parmesan cheese. Knead gently until a soft dough forms, being careful not to overwork it.
  3. Shape the ñoquis: Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion into a long log (about 1/2 inch thick), then cut them into small pieces (about 1-inch long). You can use a fork to gently press down on each piece, creating the classic ridges.
  4. Boil the ñoquis: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Drop the ñoquis into the boiling water in batches. Once they float to the surface (in about 2-3 minutes), remove them with a slotted spoon.
  5. Serve the ñoquis: In a pan, melt butter over medium heat and sauté the ñoquis for a minute or two until they’re slightly golden. Optionally, garnish with fresh basil or sage leaves.
