Tarta De Atún Recipe

Description

Tarta de Atún is a savory, delicious dish popular in many Latin American countries, especially Argentina. It's a simple yet flavorful tuna pie made with a buttery, flaky crust that holds a filling of canned tuna, onions, eggs, and often a combination of cheese and herbs. The tuna provides a light, delicate flavor that pairs perfectly with the creamy richness of the filling. The pie is easy to assemble and offers a wonderful balance of textures, with the crisp crust contrasting against the smooth, savory filling. Some variations may also include olives, capers, or a touch of paprika, adding extra depth to the dish.

Once assembled, the tarta is baked until golden brown, resulting in a warm, satisfying meal that can be enjoyed hot or cold. Perfect for a quick lunch, picnic, or dinner, tarta de atún is a versatile dish that can be served with a side salad or enjoyed on its own. Its simplicity and ease of preparation make it a go-to comfort food in many households, and its rich flavor makes it a favorite for gatherings and celebrations. Whether you're using fresh or pantry ingredients, tarta de atún is a reliable and tasty option for any occasion.

Ingridients

  • 2 cans (5 oz each) tuna in oil or water, drained
  • 1 pre-made pie crust (or homemade, if preferred)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise or sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or a mix)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for sautéing the onion)
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika (optional)
  • Olives or capers (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven: Set the oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a pie dish if not using a store-bought crust.
  2. Prepare the filling: In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and allow to cool slightly.
  3. Mix the filling: In a bowl, combine the drained tuna, sautéed onions, beaten eggs, mayonnaise or sour cream, grated cheese, and optional parsley. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika to taste.
  4. Assemble the tarta: Roll out the pie crust and fit it into the prepared pie dish. Pour the tuna filling into the crust, spreading it evenly.
  5. Bake the tarta: Place the pie in the oven and bake for about 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden and the filling is set.
  6. Serve: Let the tarta cool slightly before serving. Optionally, garnish with olives or capers for extra flavor.
